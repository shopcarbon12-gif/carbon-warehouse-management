Lightspeed Sync

Two-tab page. "Sync Engine" tab: shows when the catalog last synced successfully, how many SKUs are in the local copy, and a button to trigger a manual sync. "Live Compare" tab: runs a real-time comparison between what Lightspeed's POS says is in stock vs what RFID actually shows, highlighting discrepancies.

Sync Engine Tab

Last Success Tile & SKU Count
Status Display
Shows the timestamp of the last successful sync with Lightspeed and how many SKUs are currently in the local catalog. These are KPI-style tiles at the top of the Sync Engine tab.
Showing the last successful sync time tells admins immediately whether catalog data is fresh without having to dig into logs.
If the last sync failed (e.g., Lightspeed API was down overnight), there is no visual warning that the local catalog may be out of date. The tile shows the last success time but doesn't indicate whether the most recent sync attempt failed.
Add a "Last attempted" timestamp alongside "Last success." If the most recent attempt failed, highlight the tile in amber with an error note: "Last sync failed — 14 hours ago. Local catalog may be out of date."
Trigger Sync Button
Primary Button
Starts a manual catalog sync. Shows "Syncing…" while in progress. Appends an entry to the sync history log below when complete. Available to admins only.
Manual sync with a history log is the right approach — admins can see the result of each sync without relying on server logs.
There is no indication of how long the sync will take or how many items will be processed. Admins who trigger a sync and then navigate away have no notification when it completes.
Show estimated time ("typically 1–3 minutes") on the button and send a visible notification or badge update when the sync finishes, regardless of which page the admin is on.
Sync History Log
Log Table
A list of past sync runs with timestamp, status (success/failure), and item count. Shows the last N sync operations.
Failed syncs in the history show a status but no error detail. An admin looking at "Failed — 3 days ago" has no way to know why it failed without checking server logs externally.
Show the failure reason inline in the history table: "Failed — OAuth token expired. Go to Settings → Integrations to re-authorize." This turns a useless error entry into an actionable one.

Live Compare Tab

Live Compare Engine
Comparison Tool
Fetches current stock quantities from Lightspeed POS and compares them against RFID-tracked in-stock counts per SKU. Highlights SKUs where the counts differ. Shows a background jobs section indicating whether a compare job is running.
A live side-by-side POS vs RFID comparison is one of the most valuable features in the entire app — it directly answers "do our physical counts match our POS records?"
The comparison runs as a background job, but there is no progress indicator or estimated completion time while it's running. A large catalog comparison can take minutes, and users don't know if it's still working or stuck.
Show a live progress indicator during comparison: "Comparing SKU 234 of 1,847…" Once complete, show a summary at the top: "3 SKUs have discrepancies — 2 RFID over-count, 1 POS over-count." Link directly to the POS Compare Report for full detail.

Overall Assessment

The Live Compare feature is genuinely powerful — the ability to compare POS vs RFID counts in real time is exactly what warehouse managers need to catch inventory errors before they become customer-facing problems.
Sync failures are silent — failed syncs show a status but no reason, so admins can't self-diagnose. This needs error detail inline.
